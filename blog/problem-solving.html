Problem Solving


Got blocked on a simple problem

Being me, I over think alot and small problems are no different. Something simple as a
missing bracket or semicolon can fail code and im one to over look these problems.

Utilizing the Rubber ducky method, reviewing my code, reading error messages
console.logging and googling are ways that help me understand what im doing wrong
a good 95% of the time. Pseudocode is a great way to keep track of what you need
to do as well as leaving yourself notes if your forgetful.

Throughout the process I just felt confused, very, very confused which is great for
me atleast. Its like a drive to learn and your constatnly thinking about it and
engaging with the problem. Eventually I find what the problem is or its pointed out.
For me, Being engaged with these problems and actively trying to solve a problem on
my own helps immensely with learning or memorizing proper problem solving skills.

I think its fair to say that im still learning not only these activities in sprint 4
but from 3 as well. Ive definitely picked up the concept on alot over the past weeks
but theres so much information to retain and commands that this will be an endless
journey of learning for all of us.



Pseudocode

Using Pseudocode to leave comments or instructions on where you left of or what you still
need to do. Plain and simple.


Trying something

Theres no harm in trying something you've reflected on or found on google. Figuring out what
works and what doesnt work is better then not trying to solve the problem at all.


Rubber ducky method

Talk to something or yourself and think about what you're saying. Chances are you can figure
what it is you're overlooking. If not, you can always revise your code and compare it to code
that works and see what you've missed.
Most of the time its something simple like a bracket or semicolon ived missed.


Reading error messages

Reading error messages is Extreamly helpful to finding out whats wrong with your code.
Could be a misplaced bracket or simply that varible has already been declared.
Saying that, finding whats wrong with your code is important to make it functional and
reading your error messages is a great way of doing so.


Console.logging

If your code is failling you can console.log it you see what the cause of the error is.
Also you console.log to check the outcome of your code.


Googling

Use good google seaches to find a solution to what you're stuck on.


Asking coaches or your peers for help

Theres no shame in asking anyone for help, Were all going to struggle with material no matter what.
[1] Try and use some of the problem solving techniques/processes.
[2] Look in slack if people have struggled with the same problems and read through what helped them.
[3] Ask your facilitator for help, theyre here to help you through what ever your stuck on.
Or ask one of your peers for help.
In turn if your peers cant help or facilitator is busy or offline you could just move on and come
back to the material later on.


Improving your process with reflection

Reflect on past or present material to help sovle problems.
